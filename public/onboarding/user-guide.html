2
Adding a New Guide
1
Click "New Guide"

You'll find this button at the top of the Guide List page. Click it to open a blank guide.

2
Give your guide a title

Pick something clear and descriptive — for example: "How to Reset Your Password" or "Understanding the Insights Dashboard." Users will search for this, so keep it simple.

3
Choose a category

Pick the category that best fits the guide — Getting Started, Features, Troubleshooting, etc. This keeps the knowledge base organized.

4
Write your content

Type directly into the editor area. See Section 3 for tips on formatting your guide.

5
Save and publish

Click Save to store your work at any time. When the guide is ready to go live, change the status from Draft to Published and save again.

✅

Published guides appear immediately on the public knowledge base — no waiting, no IT request needed.

3
Using the Editor

The editor works like a simple word processor — just type and format as you go.

📝
Headings

Use headings to break your guide into sections. Highlight text and pick Heading 1 or Heading 2 from the toolbar.

📋
Bullet Lists

Great for step-by-step instructions. Click the list button in the toolbar or start a line with a dash (-).

🖼️
Images

Paste or upload screenshots directly into the editor. Images appear inline with your text.

📊
Tables

Insert a table from the toolbar to organize information in rows and columns.

💾
Auto-save history

Every time you save, a snapshot is kept. If you make a mistake, you can restore an older version.

👁️
Draft vs Published

Work privately in Draft mode. Flip to Published only when the guide is ready for users to see.

💡

Quick tip: Use Ctrl+Z (Windows) or Cmd+Z (Mac) to undo any change inside the editor, just like in Word or Google Docs.

4
Viewing Guides in the Community

All published guides from the knowledge base are streamed directly into the CrossLab Connect Community. When a guide is updated here, it updates there automatically — no copy-paste, no re-uploading.
